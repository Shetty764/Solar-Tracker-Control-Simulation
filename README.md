# Solar-Tracker-Control-Simulation
Implementation of a controller to control the axis of a solar tracker system to optimize the angle of incidence between solar panels and sunlight for maximum energy absorption.
Adding the Control System
To ensure precise control of the solar tracker, integrating a control system is essential. Begin by connecting sensors to measure the current yaw and pitch angles of the solar panels. These sensor readings serve as feedback to compare with the desired angles, which are computed by the system. Use difference blocks to find the discrepancy between the actual and desired angles, then feed this error into PID controllers for both yaw and pitch axes. The PID controllers will adjust the voltages supplied to the DC motors, which in turn, control the movement of the solar panels. This setup ensures that the panels smoothly and accurately follow the sun's trajectory, optimizing energy capture.

MATLAB Function for Calculating Yaw and Pitch Angles
Incorporate a MATLAB function to calculate the desired yaw and pitch angles based on solar position algorithms. This function, integrated within the Simulink model, will use inputs such as latitude, longitude, date, and time to compute the sun's azimuth and zenith angles. The resulting yaw and pitch angles are then used to orient the solar panels correctly. Ensure the function handles the transition between different modes of operation by providing accurate and timely updates to the control system. This approach enables the solar tracker to dynamically adjust its orientation, maximizing exposure to sunlight throughout the day.

Developing a Deep Learning Algorithm for Solar Irradiation Prediction
To further enhance the solar tracker’s performance, develop a deep learning algorithm using MATLAB’s Deep Learning Toolbox™ to predict solar irradiation levels at the installation site. Collect historical weather and solar radiation data to train the neural network model. The algorithm will analyze patterns and predict future solar irradiation, enabling the solar tracker to make more informed adjustments. By incorporating this predictive capability, the system can optimize panel orientation in anticipation of changes in sunlight intensity, thereby improving energy capture efficiency and system resilience.

Stateflow Algorithm for Energy Optimization
Design a Stateflow chart to manage the different operational modes of the solar tracker, such as Daytime, Nighttime, and Cloudy modes. The chart will transition between these states based on inputs like current time, weather conditions, and solar irradiation predictions. In Daytime mode, the system actively tracks the sun, adjusting the panels to maximize sunlight capture. Nighttime mode will park the panels in a default position to avoid unnecessary movement and wear. During Cloudy mode, the tracker will hold its current position to conserve energy and avoid unnecessary adjustments. This state-based control ensures the solar tracker operates efficiently under varying conditions, optimizing energy production and extending the system's lifespan.

Implementing Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) Algorithm
To further optimize energy capture, integrate a Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) algorithm into the solar tracker system. MPPT algorithms are designed to maximize the power output of solar panels by continuously adjusting the electrical operating point of the modules. Implementing MPPT in the Simulink model involves monitoring the voltage and current output of the solar panels and dynamically adjusting the load to ensure operation at the optimal power point. This technique is particularly beneficial in varying environmental conditions, ensuring that the solar panels always operate at their highest efficiency. By combining MPPT with the tracking system, the overall energy yield is significantly enhanced, making the solar tracker system more effective and reliable.
